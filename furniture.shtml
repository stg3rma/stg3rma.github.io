furniture

The table, chair and desk below were completed while a student in the two year Cabinet and Furniture Making program at the North Bennet Street School in Boston's North End.

Demi-lune Table

The table legs are turned and carved mahogany. The table top and sides are beautiful crotch mahogany veneer with beaded edge.

Desk on Frame

This is a William and Mary inspired piece constructed in mahagony. The legs were hand carved into double-bines ending in bun feet. The drawer front and slant-top are adorned with cherry and maple cheveron banding.

Armchair

This George III style mahagony armchair features sweeping curves, carved armrests, cabriole legs with pad feet and leather upholstered frame seat.

Materials

Wood

Hardwoods such as mahogany, maple, cherry and walnut are typically used with softwoods such as poplar and soft maple for interior elements such as drawers.

Finish

These desk and chest have been coated with tung oil. The finish is gradually padded on until a lush and luminous surface builds. Oil gives the wood a warm natural appearance but affords little protection from surface abrasion.

The chair has a shellac finish. Shellac flakes, made from a resin secreted by the lac bug, is dissolved in alchol and padded on.
