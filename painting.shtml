painting

Utilizing woodworking tools and techniques to create layered, cut and assembled acrylic paintings, these standard-sized works command a large presence relative to their somewhat diminutive size and can be read as both painting and low relief sculpture.

Mosaic #1

acrylic paint on 6" x 6" panel
In the Mosaic series the stack of dried layered paint was sliced into very thin pieces. Each piece, a cross-section of the stack it was derived from covers the painting surface.

Topography #6

acrylic paint on 6" x 6" panel
In the Topography series the layered stack of dried paint is chopped into narrow strips of varying thicknesses. The top or bottom layers from the dried layered paint stack is what is visible to the viewer. Other colors in the stack are visible in side view as pieces fill the picture plain in a manner that looks much like a topographical map.

Mosaic #5

acrylic paint on 6" x 6" panel
In this painting the paint pieces, like those in Mosaic#1, are themselves chopped even further. The small paint bits will literally poured onto a surface of acrylic gel medium.

Technique

Paint

Mix acrylic paint colors on plastic sheets and let dry.

Layer

Dried sheets of paint are layered and adhered to one another using acrylic gel medium.

Chop

The stacked sheets of acrylic paint are chopped into pieces; strips for Topography series; tiny bits for Mosaic series.

Assemble

The chopped pieces of paint are arranged and adhered to six inch wooden panels using acrylic gel medium.
